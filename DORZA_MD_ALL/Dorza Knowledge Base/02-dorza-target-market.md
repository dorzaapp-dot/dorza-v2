# Dorza — Target Market

> Last updated: March 2026 | Status: Pre-launch, early conversations in progress

## Primary market

Local small businesses in Sydney, Australia. Owner-operated or small teams (1–15 people). Typically turning over $100K–$2M annually. Time-poor, not tech-savvy, and want results without learning new tools.

## The six segments

### Tradies (plumbers, electricians, builders, landscapers)
- **Pain:** Rely entirely on word of mouth. Know they should have a website but never get around to it. Often paying for a Yellow Pages listing that does nothing.
- **Buying trigger:** A competitor shows up above them on Google. Or a customer says "I couldn't find you online."
- **What they value:** Speed, no hassle, someone who speaks plainly. They don't want a "strategy" — they want the phone to ring.
- **Price sensitivity:** Medium. Happy to pay if they see leads coming in. Sceptical of agencies due to past bad experiences.
- **Decision maker:** The owner. Decides fast, often on the spot.

### Cafes and restaurants
- **Pain:** Instagram presence is patchy. Website is outdated or non-existent. Menus change seasonally but website never gets updated. Losing customers to competitors who show up on Google Maps with good photos and reviews.
- **Buying trigger:** A new competitor opens nearby with slick branding. Or they see another cafe's Instagram and feel embarrassed by their own.
- **What they value:** Visual quality. Their food looks amazing and they want their online presence to match. Regular social media posts that make people hungry.
- **Price sensitivity:** High. Tight margins in hospitality. The $199/mo Starter tier is the sweet spot. Upsell to Growth once they see results.
- **Decision maker:** Owner or manager. May need to "think about it" — follow up within 48hrs.

### Salons and beauty (hair, nails, skin, barbershops)
- **Pain:** Rely on Instagram but posting is inconsistent. Online booking is a mess or doesn't exist. No Google reviews despite great service.
- **Buying trigger:** They see a competitor's Instagram looking polished and professional while theirs has gaps.
- **What they value:** Aesthetic quality, consistent social posting, online booking that actually works.
- **Price sensitivity:** Medium. Used to paying for quality (they sell premium services). Growth tier fits naturally.
- **Decision maker:** Owner. Often decides quickly if the visual mockup looks right.

### Fitness and wellness (gyms, yoga studios, personal trainers, physios)
- **Pain:** Class timetable buried in a PDF or Facebook post. Website is basic. Social media is sporadic motivational quotes.
- **Buying trigger:** New members say they almost didn't sign up because they couldn't find info online.
- **What they value:** Professional image, class schedules accessible online, testimonials and transformation stories on social.
- **Price sensitivity:** Variable. Personal trainers are price-sensitive. Established studios with 100+ members are comfortable with Growth/Pro tiers.
- **Decision maker:** Owner or studio manager.

### Retail shops (boutiques, florists, gift shops, specialty stores)
- **Pain:** Foot traffic is declining. Know they need an online presence but don't want to run a full e-commerce store. Google listing has wrong hours.
- **Buying trigger:** A quiet month. Or seeing a competitor pop up on Instagram.
- **What they value:** Showcasing products visually, driving foot traffic, being findable on Google Maps.
- **Price sensitivity:** Medium-high. Retail margins are tight. Starter or Growth tier.
- **Decision maker:** Owner. Often a one-person operation.

### Professional services (accountants, lawyers, consultants, real estate agents)
- **Pain:** Website looks like it was built in 2012. No content strategy. Referrals are drying up and they know they need to be findable online.
- **Buying trigger:** Losing a potential client who says "I Googled you and couldn't find anything."
- **What they value:** Credibility and professionalism. The website needs to look trustworthy. Content that positions them as experts.
- **Price sensitivity:** Low. Highest willingness to pay. Pro tier is a natural fit.
- **Decision maker:** Principal or partner. May involve a longer decision process.

## Validated learnings from early conversations

> Update this section as you have more client conversations.

**Physiotherapists (March 2026):**
- Keen for a website built quickly
- Interested in blog posts to establish expertise and help with SEO
- Social media management is appealing — they know they should post but never do
- Validates the fitness/wellness segment and the done-for-you proposition

**Restaurants (March 2026):**
- Want a website that looks as good as their food
- Social media is the primary channel they care about — Instagram especially
- Speed is a major draw — they don't want to wait weeks
- Confirms the cafe/restaurant segment and validates that social media is a strong hook

## Target suburbs (Sydney)

Prioritised by density of local businesses along walkable main streets, making canvassing efficient.

**Tier 1 — Start here:**
- Inner West: Newtown (King St), Marrickville, Leichhardt, Balmain
- Lower North Shore: Neutral Bay, Mosman, Crows Nest

**Tier 2 — Expand to:**
- Eastern Suburbs: Bondi Junction, Double Bay, Randwick
- Northern Beaches: Manly, Dee Why, Brookvale

**Tier 3 — Volume play:**
- Western Sydney: Parramatta, Bankstown, Liverpool (higher volume, more price-sensitive)

## Ideal client profile

The perfect first 20 clients look like this:
- Owner-operated business with 1–10 employees
- Located in a Tier 1 suburb
- Either has no website or a visibly outdated one
- Active on Facebook or Instagram but posting inconsistently
- No Google Business listing or an incomplete one
- Willing to pay $199–$549/month for a done-for-you service
- Responsive via text message (our primary communication channel)
