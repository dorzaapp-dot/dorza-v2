# Dorza — Sales & Go-to-Market Strategy

> Last updated: March 2026 | This is the daily playbook for the first 3–6 months.

## GTM philosophy

No paid marketing initially. Founder-led sales via in-person canvassing in high-density Sydney suburbs. The product sells itself when you can show a business owner what their website would look like and tell them it'll be live tomorrow. Referrals and partnerships scale acquisition after the first 20 clients.

## Mahir's sales positioning

Mahir brings 10 years of product experience at large media organisations. This is the credibility angle when talking to business owners:

- "I've spent a decade building digital products for some of Australia's biggest media companies. Now I'm using that experience — and the latest AI tools — to help local businesses get the same quality of digital presence, without the enterprise price tag."
- Don't lead with AI. Lead with results and speed. AI is the how, not the what.
- Local businesses buy from people they trust. Being physically present in their shop, showing genuine interest in their business, is worth more than any marketing funnel.

## The canvassing playbook

### Before you arrive

1. Google the business — check website, Google listing, Instagram, Facebook
2. Screenshot their current digital presence (before/after material)
3. Note 1–2 specific, genuine observations you can reference in conversation
4. Have the intake app loaded on your tablet

### The 60-second pitch

Walk in during a quiet period (never during lunch rush, never when they have customers waiting).

> "Hi, I'm Mahir from Dorza. We help local businesses like yours get a professional website, social media, and Google listing sorted — all done for you. I noticed [specific observation: you don't have a website / your Google listing doesn't have your current hours / your Instagram hasn't posted in a while]. We could have that sorted for you by this Friday. Can I show you what we'd build?"

Then show them an example site on your tablet for a similar business type.

### Handling objections

| They say | You say |
|----------|---------|
| "How much does it cost?" | "It starts at $199 a month — less than what most people pay their bookkeeper. And right now we're offering our first 20 clients 50% off to get started." |
| "I've been burned by agencies before" | "Totally get it. That's why we don't do lock-in contracts. Month to month. If you're not happy, you stop. But you'll have a live website tomorrow, so you'll see results fast." |
| "I don't have time for this" | "That's exactly the point — you don't have to do anything. We handle everything. All I need is 5 minutes of your time to grab some details, and we take it from there." |
| "I need to think about it" | "Of course. Here's my card. But just so you know, we're only taking 20 founding clients at the discounted rate, and we've already got [X] signed up." (Create gentle urgency.) |
| "My nephew does my website" | "No worries! If you ever want something more professional or want to get your social media running consistently, give me a shout. Here's my card." (Don't push — plant the seed.) |
| "Can you just do social media?" | "Absolutely — our Growth plan includes social media management. And the website comes included, so you get the full package." |

### After the meeting

1. Export the intake markdown from the app within 10 minutes
2. Transfer photos taken during the visit
3. Send a confirmation text immediately:

> "Hey [Name]! Great meeting you today. I'm getting started on your website now — I'll send you a preview link by [time]. Talk soon! — Mahir, Dorza"

## Canvassing targets and cadence

### Weekly targets (first 3 months)

| Activity | Target |
|----------|--------|
| Businesses visited | 20–25 per week |
| Meaningful conversations | 10–15 per week |
| Intake forms completed | 3–5 per week |
| Clients signed | 2–3 per week |

### Conversion funnel (expected)

- Walk-ins → conversation: ~60%
- Conversation → interested: ~40%
- Interested → signed: ~30%
- Overall walk-in → signed: ~7–10%

At 20 visits/week, expect 1–2 new clients per week. Reaching 20 founding clients should take 8–12 weeks.

## Target suburbs (phased)

### Phase 1 (Weeks 1–6): Inner West + Lower North Shore
These suburbs have the densest clusters of walkable small businesses.

- **Newtown (King St):** Cafes, bars, vintage shops, tattoo studios, yoga. High density, young owners, digitally aware but time-poor.
- **Marrickville:** Vietnamese restaurants, bakeries, auto shops, creative studios. Rapidly gentrifying, new businesses opening regularly.
- **Crows Nest:** Health practitioners (physios, dentists, chiros), cafes, boutiques. High foot traffic, professional clientele.
- **Neutral Bay:** Salons, fitness studios, specialty retail. Affluent area, higher willingness to pay.

### Phase 2 (Weeks 7–12): Eastern Suburbs + Northern Beaches
- Bondi Junction, Double Bay, Randwick
- Manly, Dee Why, Brookvale

### Phase 3 (Months 4–6): Western Sydney
- Parramatta, Bankstown, Liverpool
- Higher volume, more price-sensitive. Lean on Starter tier.

## Referral program

Launch after month 2, once you have happy clients who can vouch for the service.

- Existing clients get $100 off their next month for every referral that signs up
- No cap on referrals
- Referral tracking: simple spreadsheet initially, formalise later
- Local business owners know other local business owners — this is the highest-ROI channel

## Founding client strategy

The first 20 clients are the foundation. They provide testimonials, case studies, before/after material, and word-of-mouth referrals. Treat them exceptionally well.

1. **Over-deliver on speed.** Website live in 12 hours, not 24. First social posts within 48 hours.
2. **Over-communicate.** Text them at every milestone. "Your Google listing is live!" "Your first post went out today!"
3. **Ask for feedback early.** "Is there anything you'd change?" shows you care and catches issues before they fester.
4. **Photograph the before/after.** Screenshot their old website (or lack thereof), then screenshot the new one. This is your most powerful sales tool for the next 50 clients.
5. **Get the testimonial at the 2-week mark.** Don't wait months. They're most excited right after launch.
6. **Ask for a Google review.** A 5-star review from a real business owner is gold for Dorza's own credibility.
